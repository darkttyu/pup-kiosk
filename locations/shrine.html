Apolinario Mabini Shrine

The Apolinario Mabini Shrine at PUP Sta. Mesa is a historical landmark dedicated to the memory of Apolinario Mabini, one of the most important figures in Philippine history. Mabini, known as the "Brains of the Revolution," was a key leader and intellectual during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule and the subsequent Philippine-American War. The shrine honors his contributions to the nation's independence and his unwavering commitment to justice, freedom, and national sovereignty.

The shrine is located within the university's grounds, symbolizing PUP's commitment to preserving and celebrating Filipino history and heritage. It serves as a place of reflection and learning, offering students, faculty, and visitors a chance to connect with the life and legacy of Mabini. The site is not only a reminder of his significant role in shaping the nation’s history but also an educational resource, helping to instill a deeper understanding of Philippine values, patriotism, and the sacrifices made for the country’s freedom.

In addition to its historical significance, the Apolinario Mabini Shrine is also a peaceful space for contemplation. It offers visitors an opportunity to reflect on the life of a national hero while surrounded by the lush greenery of the campus. The shrine plays an important role in promoting historical consciousness among students and the PUP community, reminding them of the university’s roots and its commitment to producing future leaders who are mindful of the country’s history and the principles of service to the nation.
